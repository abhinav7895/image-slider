Lucknow

Lucknow, a historic city in northern India, is renowned for its rich cultural heritage. The capital of the state of Uttar Pradesh, Lucknow, is famous for its Mughal and Awadhi influences The city boasts iconic landmarks like the Bara Imambara and Rumi Darwaza, as well as a vibrant street food scene, making it a captivating destination for travelers seeking a taste of India's past and present.

Mumbai

Mumbai, India's bustling metropolis, is a captivating blend of tradition and modernity. Known as the "City of Dreams," it houses Bollywood, the heart of India's film industry, and boasts a diverse cultural tapestry. Kolkata

Kolkata, the cultural capital of India, is a vibrant metropolis on the banks of the Hooghly River. Known for its rich history, it was the capital of British India until 1911. The city boasts an array of colonial-era architecture, bustling markets, and a thriving arts scene. Kolkata is famous for its Durga Puja celebrations, literary heritage, and delectable street food.

New Delhi

New Delhi, India's vibrant capital, is a dynamic blend of ancient history and modernity. Home to iconic landmarks like the majestic Red Fort, India Gate, and the Lotus Temple, the city showcases diverse cultures, cuisines, and traditions. Its bustling markets, such as Chandni Chowk, offer a sensory delight, while government institutions like the Parliament and Rashtrapati Bhavan symbolize the nation's governance.

Dehradun

Dehradun, nestled in the Indian state of Uttarakhand, is a picturesque city known for its lush greenery and serene landscapes. It serves as the gateway to the Himalayas and offers a perfect blend of natural beauty and urban life. Dehradun is renowned for its prestigious educational institutions, pleasant climate, and historic landmarks like the Forest Research Institute.
